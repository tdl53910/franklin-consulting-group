# Franklin Consulting Group

**Franklin Consulting Group (FCG)** is a student-led consulting organization established through the Student Government Association of the University of Georgia and affiliated with the Franklin College of Arts and Sciences. The organization provides structured, client-based experiential learning, applied research, and interdisciplinary project opportunities for undergraduate students.

FCG is designed to complement and strengthen existing experiential learning initiatives by creating a centralized, accessible framework that connects students with real-world projects aligned to their academic skill sets.

---

## Mission

The mission of Franklin Consulting Group is to expand access to applied learning opportunities for Franklin College students by facilitating interdisciplinary, solution-oriented projects that benefit students, faculty, university departments, and external partners.

Through hands-on engagement, FCG aims to enhance career readiness, promote professional development, and bridge classroom learning with practical application.

---

## What We Do

Franklin Consulting Group organizes semester-based project cohorts that work on applied initiatives such as:

- Website and student resource development  
- Institutional and departmental support projects  
- Applied research assistance across the natural and social sciences  
- Data-driven analysis and technical development  
- Miscellaneous client-based initiatives aligned with student expertise  

Projects are sourced from faculty members, university departments, campus partners, and external organizations seeking structured undergraduate involvement.

## Students

Students selected for Franklin Consulting Group cohorts gain:

- Hands-on experience working on real projects
- Exposure to interdisciplinary collaboration
- Professional skill development in applied settings
- Opportunities to work directly with faculty, administrators, and partners

Applications are open to all Franklin College students and are evaluated based on skill alignment, interest, and project needs.
